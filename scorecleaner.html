ScoreCleaner

(July 2020 - Present)
At present, in my free time I am building an application that aimed at solving a "review bombing" problem. Some groups of people massively leave negative user reviews for a published work, for example, video game or film, to harm its sales and popularity. This is often done to draw wider attention to political or cultural issues around the product or its vendor. Unfortunately, online media review aggregates do not have any mechanisms to prevent or regulate this occurrence. This application extracts user reviews from Metacritic for a given product and then processes these data, analyses it, and detects "angry" reviews. In the end, it shows average user score free from "review bombing", a list of adequate reviews and additional statistics with visualization which Metacritic does not provide. Although this application works now, I believe that in future it can be significantly improved, for example, by using natural language processing for more precise recognition and analysis of "angry reviews" and defining the origin and the nature of the phenomenon in a particular case.
